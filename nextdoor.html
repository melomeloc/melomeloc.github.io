Nextdoor's New Service - the Speaker Series

Nextdoor's Speaker Series

Conceptual Project

April, 2016

Overview

This is a conceptual class project. We imagined Nextdoor, a community social network, was going to add what they called the Speaker Series, a service that allows a community member to open up their homes to host talks by thought leaders.

Team

I worked alongside with another teammate. My role was the UX designer with additional focus on UI & Visual Design.

Tools & Methods

Concept design; Interaction design for mobile app; Survey Design; User interviews; Affinity Mapping; Journey Mapping

The Problem

“It hurts to think that my guests are not enjoying my party!”

People who are interested in hosting events at their homes are dissuaded because they feel anxious that guests won’t have a good time given the lack of time, resources or knowledge of hosting.

The Solution

Assist users to host a talk from end to end.

Our approach is to add a flow on the existing Nextdoor mobile app to assist hosts along their journey of hosting talks at home. This would reduce the amount of time and energy needed for planning, organizing and hosting talks at home. Most importantly, it will allow them to enjoy the intellectual exchange with thought leaders and neighbors without feeling anxious.

The Discovery

Identify the roadblocks we need to remove for hosts.

GET TO KNOW THE HOSTS.
We sent out a survey to hosts & guests as well as interviewed 6 people who were hosts or potential hosts to understand their painpoints and motivations.

PAINPOINTS & MOTIVATIONS.
With the data we collected from interviews and survey, we used a method called affinity map to distill them into three key insights that informed our design in the later stage:

  1. Forming deeper connection with neighbors over shared interest is the main motivation for hosting a community event at home.
  2. People have trouble managing logistics of hosting community events.
  3. People have anxiety over how to provide a delightful experience for guests.

YOUNG FUTURE THOUGHT LEADER vs. BUSY WORKING PROFESSIONAL
We created two personas based on the distinct traits of users found in research.

JOURNEY MAP.
Based on the user stories of the two personas, we created a journey map to look at the experience of a host throughout their journey of hosting a talk from awareness to during-event management. It helped us to identify opportunities to assist hosts in each stage of the journey.

Deliverables

All features designed to remove friction from hosting experience.

SEARCH FOR SPEAKER BASED ON INTEREST.

Research showed that hosts will most likely to host and attendees would most likely to attend a talk if the topic interest them. Therefore, we created a UI that allows users to search for a speaker by theme.

EVENT CREATION FLOW DESIGNED TO ADDRESS USERS’ CONCERNS.

PRE-EVENT MANAGEMENT FLOW FOR USERS TO KEEP TRACK OF
UPDATES AND ACTION ITEMS BEFORE THE TALK.

- DURING THE TALK -

EVENT MANAGEMENT UI DURING THE TALK
FOR HOSTS TO STAY ORGANIZED.

Future Plan

These are several future steps that Nextdoor can take to improve users’ experience even further:

  1. New service: Dine with a thought leader

    When we looked at the journey map, we noticed there’s an opportunity to remove the hosts’ uncertainties by allowing the them to get familiar with the speakers. So recommended some research and testing on an idea we called “dine with a thought leader” where interested hosts can sign up to have a round table discussion with the speaker.

  2. Research on attendees.

    We believe more research on the attendees it will definitely increase the overall experience of Nextdoor’s Speaker Series.
