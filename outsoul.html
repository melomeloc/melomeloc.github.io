Outsoul Discovery Flow

Trip finding flow redesigned to inspire more Outdoor Adventure

Outsoul Discovery Flow

Client:Outsoul

www.outsoul.com

May, 2016

Overview

Founders at Outsoul had asked us to redesign its existing UI to make it easier for people to discover and book trips on the site.

Team & Duration

I worked alongside with 2 other designers on this project for 2.5 weeks. My focus was on research, UI Design and content strategy.

Tools & Methods

Interface Design in SketchApp; User Interview; Comparative/Competitive Analysis; Research planning; Journey Mapping; User personas; Usability testing.

The Problem

“Booking a guided trip is boring and complicated, I don’t want to deal with it at all!”

When Outsoul approached us, its bounce rate on landing page was high and it was only a place where Outdoor Adventures providers listed their businesses. If people wanted to book a guided trip, they would still need to go through many steps before finding a trusted guide. Feeling uninspired, many people dropped off from the site.

Our Approach

The excitement of adventure should begin before the actual trip!

Our approach was to draw users into a story of adventure while they are browsing fluidly for guided trips. There were two strategies we used to illustrate our approach:

  1. A content strategy and visual design that keep user inspired from the moment they land on the homepage to booking trips.
  2. An intuitive and streamlined user flow for user to compare and book trips without frustration.

The Discovery

“I seek self-discovery and I want to look cool!”

Get to know the Outdoor Adventurers

We surveyed about a hundred people, sifted through secondary data and conducted 6 in-person interviews with people who had used Outdoor Adventure (OA) guides as well as people who were interested in using one. These methods helped us to uncover some important insights from Outdoor Adventurers:

  1. The two underlying motivations for people to do Outdoor Adventures are pushing personal boundaries and impressing others.
  2. Good pictures often to spark their interest in doing an Outdoor Adventure trip.
  3. When choosing a trip to book, the most important deciding factors are price, reviews from others and the quality of guides.
  4. People aren’t feeling inspired by the original website.
Create Personas

We then distilled the research data using a method called affinity mapping. The result was the profiles of two types of targeted users (as shown below), a hardcore outdoor adventurer and a free spirited millennial. These personas helped to inform the kinds of story we needed to tell. In addition, we also created two journey maps to identify the points in which Outsoul can help them throughout their journey.

Find our direction

We identified some competitors / comparable businesses and performed an analysis. The three key things we looked at were:

  1. Market (Leisure travel vs Adventure travel)
  2. The organization of the information on their websites (Organized vs Disorganized)
  3. Branding (Inspiring vs dull)

This analysis helped us to determine that Outsoul would be an adventure oriented trip-booking site that is organized and inspiring.

Deliverables

Every detail designed to prime users for an adventure as well as to inspire them to get outdoor.

A Thrilling Landing Page

We believe that moment users land on the homepage, they should already feel the thrill of adventure. Therefore, we place an image slider on top to showcase the actions users can accomplish with Outsoul. And the filters right below would allow them to discover further.

  1. Hero Images
    Images in the slider contains action shots that help users to visualize the exciting moments of the outdoor trips.
  2. Headline
    A headline using language related to adventure or aiming to inspire thrill-seeking experiences. In this case, the word “discover” inspires adventure.
  3. Section Title
    Same as #2, the word “explore” aims to inspire for an adventure.
  4. Call-to-Action
    The very discoverable Call-to-Action button is in golden-yellow. It brings an energetic and adventurous spirit to the design.

Curated Trips Based on Users' Stories

User research shows that often times people are motivated to get outdoor because they are inspired by others’ “cool stories”. When we were brainstorming for ideas, we asked the question: “What if we provide the themes of the stories, then let the users to create the actions and dialogues?” The result of that was the lists on the right. They are curated based on the stories that inspire our users to help them discover trips easier.

Find Trips based on Adventure Style

Not interested in trips that are not challenging? No problem! Users can easily narrow down their search results to only trips that are for hardcore outdoor adventurers.

Trip Tiles Provide the most Relevant Information

Based on the deciding factors of booking a trip, we prioritized information on the trip tiles to help users to find their their favorite trips in the most efficient way possible.

Future Recommendation

A positive first step but more things remain to be improved!

As of writing this, Outsoul was still working on building the backend functions that would allow users to book guided trips on Outsoul directly. While handing off the deliverables to Outsoul, we also recommended a few next steps to them, that included:

  1. conducting indepth research on the users of each individual activity offered on Outsoul because there are clear differences between users of these activities.
  2. offering pre-trip as well as post-trip services (instead of just booking service) so that Outsoul can have full control of the quality of the whole user experience.
