Material UI Design Challenges

Personal Project

August, 2016 - Present

Overview

This project is to design an UI element per day using Material Design Principle for 100 days. The UI prompts are provided by Daily UI. I will update this page regularly until I complete all 100 challenges.

Goals

I have 2 main goals for this project:

  1. To study closely the user & business goals of different UI elements.
  2. To get myself 100% familiar with Material Design Principle.

Tools & Methods

Material UI Design; Sketch; Visual Design

  • #001 - Sign Up Form

Day 1 - Sign Up Form

The goals of a SignUp Form vary depending on the type of businesses. For example, if a company is trying to generate quality leads, one of its goals would be to ask a lot of specific questions to make sure the customers are the right fit for their products. However, the Sign Up form that most businesses need today have only a few common goals:

  1. to gather user data for marketing reason
  2. to recognize users as the owner of some data
  3. to keep sensitive data of the users safe

For the simplicity of this exercise, I am going to design the more common type of Sign Up form with the above goals.

These a few rules that inform my design decisions on this Sign Up form:

  1. Most users only give your form very little attention (rumor says 8 seconds!). Therefore, the form should take users as little time as possible. In my design,
    • users can save time and effort by signing up with their social media account
    • users are only asked for their email address and password if they choose not to sign up via social media accounts
    • the helper text under the password field saves users' time from creating password in a wrong format
  2. People refuse to sign up because they are concerned about spamming. (Don't you hate that [Company of your choice] sends you notifications 3 times a day about some updates that you have no time to care about?) In my design,
    • the subtext under the main title aims to let users know that they don't have to worry about being spammed
